## Philosophy

These are smart professionals who keep a 108-room hotel running at 9.4 on Booking.com. The problem isn't intelligence. It's that nobody ever taught them patiently, and every IT initiative has felt like "one more thing dumped on us" (see: Ирина's "это нереально сделать сейчас").

**Three rules for whoever delivers this training:**
1. Never touch their device without explaining what you're doing and why.
2. Make them do it with their hands, not watch you do it.
3. Celebrate when it works. Don't sigh when it doesn't.

**Delivery format:** 1-on-1 or max 3 people. 15-20 minute sessions at their workstation. Never in a classroom. Never as homework.

## Tier 1: Non-Negotiable Basics (Week 1-2)

**Goal:** Every manager can log in, find things, and not panic when something looks different.

**Time commitment:** Two 15-minute sessions per person over 2 weeks.

### 1.1 Passwords & Logins

| Skill | Tool | Time | Verification |
|-------|------|------|-------------|
| Understand what a password manager is and why it exists (not "another app to install" but "never remember passwords again") | Keeper | 5 min | Can explain it to a colleague in one sentence |
| Open Keeper on their device and find a saved password | Keeper | 5 min | Find and copy the password for one service they use daily |
| Log into a service using a password from Keeper (not from a Post-it note) | Keeper + any service | 5 min | Log into Bitrix24 or Travelline using Keeper-stored password |
| Know who to contact when locked out (Semen, not "ask a colleague for their login") | Process | 2 min | Can name the person and the channel (Telegram) |
| Change their own password when prompted | Any service | 5 min | Walk through one real password change |

**Why this first:** The Dec 28 fraud incident (14,750 RUB loss) happened because of shared credentials. This is money, not theory.

### 1.2 Finding & Opening Files

| Skill | Tool | Time | Verification |
|-------|------|------|-------------|
| Open a link sent via Telegram or email (in the browser, not asking someone to print it) | Browser | 3 min | Click a shared Google Drive link and see the document |
| Download a file from Google Drive to their computer | Google Drive | 5 min | Download the Owner Dashboard spreadsheet |
| Find a file they downloaded (understand the Downloads folder) | File system | 5 min | Locate the file they just downloaded without help |
| Open an Excel/Google Sheets file and scroll through it | Google Sheets / Excel | 5 min | Open the Owner Dashboard and find this month's occupancy number |
| Understand the difference between "view only" and "can edit" (and not panic when they can't edit something) | Google Drive | 3 min | Try to edit a view-only doc, read the message, understand it's normal |

### 1.3 Basic Communication Hygiene

| Skill | Tool | Time | Verification |
|-------|------|------|-------------|
| Reply to an email (not forward, not new message) | Yandex 360 / Gmail | 3 min | Reply to a test email from Semen |
| Forward a document to a specific person by email | Email | 3 min | Forward a file to a colleague by entering their email address |
| Send a file in Telegram (not just photos, but documents/PDFs) | Telegram | 3 min | Send a PDF to Semen via Telegram |
| Know when to use Telegram vs email (quick operational = Telegram, formal/documented = email) | Process | 2 min | Can state the rule |

### 1.4 "Something Went Wrong" Protocol

| Skill | Tool | Time | Verification |
|-------|------|------|-------------|
| Take a screenshot on their device (phone or computer) | Device | 5 min | Take a screenshot and send it to Semen |
| Describe a problem clearly: what they were trying to do, what happened instead, screenshot attached | Telegram | 5 min | Write one problem report that Semen can act on without follow-up questions |
| Restart a program (close fully and reopen, not just minimize) | Any | 3 min | Close and reopen their browser |
| Know that "it says something in English" is not a crisis. Read the screen. Look for familiar words or buttons. | Any | 3 min | Encounter an English dialog box and identify the "OK" or "Cancel" button |

## Tier 3: Independence (Month 2-3)

**Goal:** Can solve basic problems alone, help a colleague, and adopt a new tool without anxiety.

**Time commitment:** One 30-minute session per person per week over 4-6 weeks.

### 3.1 Self-Troubleshooting

| Skill | Tool | Time | Verification |
|-------|------|------|-------------|
| Clear browser cache/cookies when a website isn't working right | Browser | 10 min | Walk through clearing cache, explain when to try this |
| Recognize common error types: "no internet" vs "wrong password" vs "server down" | Any | 15 min | Show 5 common error screens, can categorize 4 out of 5 correctly |
| Use incognito/private mode to test if a problem is browser-specific | Browser | 10 min | Open same page in normal and incognito, compare results |
| Restart the VPN (HitVPN) when it drops | HitVPN | 5 min | Disconnect and reconnect VPN, confirm services are reachable |
| Check if a website is down for everyone or just them (use downdetector.ru or ask a colleague) | Browser | 5 min | When Bitrix doesn't load, check if it's just them before reporting |

### 3.2 Working with New Tools

| Skill | Tool | Time | Verification |
|-------|------|------|-------------|
| Install a program or app from a link (without panicking about permissions dialogs) | Any device | 15 min | Install one app they haven't used before (e.g., a test app) |
| Understand that "Allow access" dialogs are normal. Camera, microphone, notifications. Know which to accept and which to decline. | Any device | 10 min | Encounter 3 permission prompts, make reasonable decisions |
| Sign up for a new service using Keeper to generate and store the password | Keeper + any | 15 min | Create an account somewhere new, password saved in Keeper from step one |
| Watch a Loom/video tutorial and follow along (pause, do, resume) | Loom / YouTube | 15 min | Complete one Loom training video for a tool they haven't used |
| Read a simple help article (Google "how to [task] in [tool]") | Browser | 10 min | Solve one small problem by searching for the answer |

### 3.3 Helping Others

| Skill | Tool | Time | Verification |

## Implementation Plan

### Who Delivers the Training

**Primary trainer: Semen** (with supervision from Ed/Dmitry on curriculum).
**Backup/peer trainer: Лилия** (most tech-capable staff member, self-adopted AI tools).
**Quality check: Ludmila** confirms department heads are progressing.

### Realistic Schedule

Semen is at 20 hrs/week. Training cannot consume more than 4 hrs/week of his time, or operational IT breaks.

| Week | Focus | Sessions | Who |
|------|-------|----------|-----|
| 1 | Tier 1.1-1.2 (passwords, files) | 2 sessions x 5 people (stagger) | Semen |
| 2 | Tier 1.3-1.4 (communication, troubleshooting) | 2 sessions x 5 people | Semen |
| 3-4 | Tier 2.1-2.2 (Google Drive, Sheets) | 2 sessions/week x 5 people | Semen |
| 5-6 | Tier 2.3-2.5 (Keeper, Telegram, role-specific) | 2 sessions/week, role-grouped | Semen + Лилия |
| 7-10 | Tier 3 (independence, troubleshooting, security) | 1 session/week per person | Semen + Лилия |

**Total elapsed time:** 10 weeks (2.5 months).
**Total trainer time:** ~50-60 hours.
**Per-person time investment:** ~6-8 hours spread over 10 weeks.

### How to Track Progress

Simple Google Sheet. One row per person, one column per skill. Three states:

- Empty = not started
- Дата = trained on that date
- ✓ = verified (can do it independently one week later)

No grades. No scoring. No ranking. Just: can they do it or not yet.

### Critical Success Factors

1. **Start with Keeper.** The fraud incident is still fresh. Everyone understands why passwords matter. This gives the training immediate credibility.

2. **Svetlana goes first (or at least early).** When the Owner participates, it signals that this isn't beneath anyone. Short, focused session. Google Sheets dashboard only.

3. **Never batch more than 3 people per session.** People won't ask questions in front of peers if they feel behind.

4. **Build on what they already do well.** Ludmila already uses Telegram effectively. Start her Telegram tier from what she knows and add. Don't re-teach basics she has.

5. **Hardware blockers must be solved first.** SPA team has no shared smartphone. Natalia is on a personal laptop. If people don't have devices, training is theater. Budget: 2-3 smartphones for departments without shared devices (~30-50K RUB total).

6. **Celebrate the first "I figured it out myself" moment.** That's the real graduation, not any checklist.
